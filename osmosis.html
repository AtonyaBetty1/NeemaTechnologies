What is reverse osmosis water purification?
- Reverse osmosis removes contaminants from unfiltered water, or feed water, when pressure forces it through a semipermeable membrane.
- Water flows from the more concentrated side (more contaminants) of the RO membrane to the less concentrated side (fewer contaminants) to provide clean drinking water.
- The fresh water produced is called the permeate. The concentrated water left over is called the waste or brine.

- A semipermeable membrane has small pores that block contaminants but allow water molecules to flow through.
- In osmosis, water becomes more concentrated as it passes through the membrane to obtain equilibrium on both sides.
- Reverse osmosis, however, blocks contaminants from entering the less concentrated side of the membrane.
- For example, when pressure is applied to a volume of saltwater during reverse osmosis, the salt is left behind and only clean water flows through.
